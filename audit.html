Scoring Formula

Overall Score (out of 10)

(AI Awareness × 0.67) + (Business Readiness × 0.33)
AI Awareness
6 questions  ·  67% weight
Business Readiness
3 questions  ·  33% weight
Section 1 — AI Awareness (67% of score)
AI Awareness
How familiar is this person with AI tools today?
6 questions · 1–10 each
Question 1
Have you used any AI tools — like ChatGPT, Claude, or similar — in your work or personal life?
If yes: Which ones? How often? What for?
If no: Have you heard of them? Any curiosity there?
Script note (don't read aloud): If they use Microsoft 365 — ask whether they've noticed a "Copilot" button appearing in Word, Excel, Outlook, or Teams. Many businesses have it and don't realise. Probe whether they've tried it or ignored it.
Scoring guide
1–2 Never heard of them or actively avoids
3–4 Heard of them, tried once, no habit
5–6 Uses occasionally for personal tasks
7–8 Uses regularly, work and personal
9–10 Daily use, multiple tools, intentional
Question 2
When you think about AI, what's your gut reaction — excited, nervous, sceptical, or somewhere else?
Probe: What drives that feeling? Has it changed recently?
Scoring guide
1–3 Fearful, resistant, dismissive
4–5 Sceptical but curious
6–7 Open and interested
8–10 Excited, energised, already leaning in
Question 3
Have you ever tried to use AI for a specific task in your business — even informally or just once?
If yes: What task? How did it go? Did you keep using it?
If no: What's stopped you?
Scoring guide
1–3 Never tried, not sure where to start
4–5 Tried once, struggled, abandoned
6–7 Tried a few times with some success
8–10 Regular use, tangible outcomes
Question 4
If you could wave a wand and have AI handle one part of your business — what would it be?
Probe: Why that one? How much time does it take you right now? How much does it cost you?
Scoring guide
1–3 Can't identify anything specific
4–5 Vague idea, not articulated clearly
6–7 Clear problem, not sure AI can solve it
8–10 Sharp, specific, high-value target
Question 5
Is anyone else in your team using AI right now — formally or informally?
Probe: Who? What for? Do you know about it or is it under the radar?
Scoring guide
1–3 No one, no awareness
4–5 Maybe one person, ad hoc
6–7 A few people using it informally
8–10 Team actively using it, some structure
Question 6
What's your biggest hesitation or concern about bringing AI into the business?
Probe: Is it about trust, cost, time, staff, data, or something else?
Scoring guide — score inversely (more concern = lower)
1–3 Deep, blocking concerns, highly resistant
4–5 Real concerns but willing to explore
6–7 Mild concerns, practical mindset
8–10 Minimal hesitation, ready to go
Section 2 — Business Readiness (33% of score)
Business Readiness
Is this business set up to adopt AI without major friction?
3 questions · 1–10 each
Question 7
What does your tech setup look like right now — what tools, software, or systems do you run the business on?
Probe: Cloud-based or on-premise? Do people have their own devices and logins?
Scoring guide
1–3 Mostly paper, no cloud, fragmented
4–5 Some cloud, mixed, not unified
6–7 Good cloud setup, people have logins
8–10 Well-organised, modern stack, ready
Question 8
How is your business information organised — things like SOPs, templates, customer info, team knowledge?
Probe: Is it written down? Searchable? In people's heads?
Scoring guide
1–3 In people's heads, nothing written
4–5 Some docs, not organised or current
6–7 Reasonable docs, reasonably accessible
8–10 Well-documented, searchable, up to date
Question 9
How do you think your team would respond to learning and using a new AI tool?
Probe: Is there a "we don't do new things" culture, or are people generally open to change?
Scoring guide
1–3 Strong resistance expected, entrenched culture
4–5 Mixed — some open, some not
6–7 Mostly open, might need gentle managing
8–10 Team is energised and ready to go
Outcome Bands — What the Score Means
1 – 3
Early Stage
Very early. Little to no exposure to AI. Business may need foundational groundwork before Day One delivers full value. Be honest — consider whether Day One is premature or whether the audit outcome is to start smaller.
4 – 5
Aware but Not Moving
Knows AI matters, hasn't acted. Classic "overwhelmed by the noise" profile. Day One is the right move — they need a guided, practical start to get unstuck. High conversion potential.
6 – 7
Ready to Go
Some exposure, clear motivation, business can absorb change. The strongest Day One candidate. Quick to activate, likely to see fast wins. May be ready for Day Two conversation early.
8 – 10
Already Moving
Strong awareness and readiness. Day One will confirm and accelerate. Day Two conversation may be appropriate even before Day One wraps. Consider whether they need the full Day One or whether you can fast-track.
